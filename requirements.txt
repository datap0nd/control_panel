# ERP Revenue and Cross-Source Mapping Review

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

## Purpose

This document records the validated ERP/P&L lineage, current temporary reporting decisions, and unresolved business-definition and source-mapping questions. It should be updated when source owners confirm the outstanding items.

## Validated ERP/P&L lineage

The P&L semantic model contains a genuine monthly financial fact table. It is not equivalent to the current dashboard placeholder sourced from `sales.csv`.

- Fact table: `P&L`
- Native grain: month, combined company/subsidiary, P&L line, scenario, item, segment, and market name
- Subsidiary field: `Combined Company`
- Actual scenario labels: `CY` and `LY`
- Plan scenario label: `Plan`
- Gross revenue candidate: `Gross Sales Amt`
- Net revenue candidate: `Net Sales Amt`
- 2026 Gross Sales is reconstructed as Net Sales plus Sales Deduction.
- The 2026 ingestion multiplies source values by 1,000,000 to restore base units.
- SGE, SETK, and SESAR map directly to dashboard subsidiaries.

## ERP/P&L blockers

1. The model removes the source `Version` field before scenario assignment and labels every 2026 row as `CY`.
2. August through December 2026 contain nonblank values classified as CY actual. These cannot be actual results as of the review date and are likely forecast, plan, or another version.
3. July 2026 is an open month and should not be treated as a completed monthly ERP actual without owner confirmation.
4. It is not confirmed whether dashboard ERP Revenue should mean Gross Sales or Net Sales. Net Sales is the recommended financial-revenue definition unless the business explicitly requires Gross Sales.
5. It is not confirmed whether the KPI should show the entire subsidiary P&L or only the program-specific portion.
6. If program-only revenue is required, an approved mapping using ItemPart, Segment, Market Name, or another ERP key is required.
7. The extracted model does not expose a currency field. The owner must confirm that values for all included subsidiaries are in one additive reporting currency.
8. ERP actual and plan should use matching P&L definitions and month windows. The unrelated dashboard revenue-plan file should not be compared with ERP actuals without a formal reconciliation.

## Temporary reporting decisions

- Do not label `Sales[gross_revenue]` or `Sales[subsidiary_revenue]` as ERP revenue.
- Keep the fake ERP KPI disabled or clearly relabeled until the P&L source is integrated.
- For a monthly ERP metric, use completed months only. As of this review, that means January through June 2026.
- If ERP Revenue is approved as Net Sales, use `Net Sales Amt` for actual, plan, LY, and attainment.
- If Gross Sales is required, use `Gross Sales Amt` consistently for actual, plan, LY, and attainment.
- Filter the initial ERP KPI to SGE, SETK, and SESAR unless additional companies are explicitly brought into dashboard scope.

## Cross-source mapping status

### Sales to Sales Plan

- Sales actual channels include B2B, E-Store, Operator, Others, Retail, and SES.
- Sales Plan channels include E-Store, Operator, Retail, SES, and Standalone.
- `Others -> Standalone` has been confirmed.
- B2B still has no approved Sales Plan equivalent.
- Channel-level plan reporting is therefore not fully reconcilable.
- The current safe comparison grain is month plus subsidiary, with all plan channels included.
- `sales_plan.csv` uses `Sept`, while some model logic expects `Sep`. The source or ingestion logic must accept one canonical September label.

### Sales to Sellout for attach rate

- E-Store is the only confidently matched channel across Sales and Sellout.
- The sources do not currently provide a reliable shared account, partner, store, or channel key for the remaining channels.
- Sales account/partner detail is mostly blank or `NA` and cannot support a deeper automatic join.
- The existing detailed channel map uses channel code, partner, and store keys that are not available at usable quality in Sales actuals.
- Separate Retail, Operator, SES, B2B, and Others attach rates are unsupported until an authoritative crosswalk is supplied.
- The temporary safe attach-rate grain is month plus subsidiary.
- At subsidiary grain, attach rate is summed Sales quantity divided by the corresponding summed Sellout denominator. Rates must never be averaged.

### Attach-rate denominator and export logic

- Raw attach rate should use total eligible Sellout as its denominator.
- Excluding-export attach rate should use the retained or non-export Sellout denominator.
- The model needs an owner-approved definition of how export units are identified.
- An account-level export list or equivalent source flag is not available in the current extracts.
- The terms `S/O That Stays`, retained Sellout, and excluding-export Sellout must be confirmed as equivalent or documented as separate concepts.

### Sales metric and partner reporting

- Use the Sales table for sales metrics whenever the requested grain exists there.
- Use New Policies only as an explicitly labelled proxy when partner-level sales are unavailable from Sales.
- The Sales extract does not currently provide reliable partner-level sales for every reporting need.
- An owner-approved partner mapping and source precedence rule are still required.

### Monthly paid subscribers

- Monthly paid subscribers by subsidiary begins in February 2026 in the available data.
- Earlier months have no qualifying paid rows even when broader policy history exists.
- The source owner must confirm whether this reflects a genuine product launch, a source-system migration, a missing extraction period, or an incomplete payment-type mapping.

## Required owner-provided crosswalk

The preferred controlled mapping file should contain:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Source table | Identifies Sales, Sellout, Plan, Policy, or ERP |
| Subsidiary | Common subsidiary key |
| Raw channel | Source-specific channel value |
| Raw channel code | Stable source code where available |
| Raw account/partner | Source-specific account or partner |
| Raw store | Source-specific store where available |
| Canonical channel | Approved reporting channel |
| Plan channel | Approved Sales Plan equivalent |
| Export flag | Owner-approved export classification |
| Effective start | Mapping validity start |
| Effective end | Mapping validity end |
| Owner/status | Approval and stewardship |
