1. Open policy.csv in Excel.

2. Click any cell in the data and press Ctrl+T. Confirm "My table has headers" and click OK.

3. Add a new column at the far right named:
Cancellation Date Parsed

Enter this formula in the first data row. Excel should fill the entire table column automatically:
=IF([@cancellation_date]="","",IF(ISNUMBER([@cancellation_date]),INT([@cancellation_date]),IFERROR(LET(x,TRIM([@cancellation_date]),p1,FIND("/",x),p2,FIND("/",x,p1+1),DATE(VALUE(MID(x,p2+1,4)),VALUE(LEFT(x,p1-1)),VALUE(MID(x,p1+1,p2-p1-1)))),"")))

This supports both real Excel dates and text dates written as M/D/YYYY or MM/DD/YYYY. If cancellation_date is already a real Excel date, it keeps the existing date serial instead of parsing that serial as text.

Format this new column as Short Date. For example, 1/28/2025 must remain 1/28/2025. Do not continue if it becomes a large year or a number such as 1388995.

4. Add another column named:
Product Monthly Amount Parsed

Enter:
=IFERROR(NUMBERVALUE([@product_monthly_amount],".",","),0)

5. Add another column named:
June 2026 Numerator

Enter:
=--AND(ISNUMBER([@[Cancellation Date Parsed]]),[@[Product Monthly Amount Parsed]]>0,[@[Cancellation Date Parsed]]>=DATE(2026,6,1),[@[Cancellation Date Parsed]]<DATE(2026,7,1))

6. Click anywhere in the table. Go to Insert > PivotTable > From Table/Range > New Worksheet.

7. In PivotTable Fields:
- Drag payment_type_code to Rows.
- Drag June 2026 Numerator to Values.
- Open Value Field Settings and select Sum, not Count.

Expected result:
- payment_type_code 2 = 20
- Grand Total = 20

Do not count policy_id. This numerator is a row count, and the helper column contributes one only when the row has a positive monthly amount and a June 2026 cancellation date.
