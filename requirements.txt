### 1. ERP integration

Do not replace the existing Gross Revenue card with ERP. Operational Sales revenue and financial ERP revenue are different facts.

There are two legitimate ERP products:

- **Financial context, recommended MVP:** Subsidiary ERP Net Sales, Plan, attainment, YoY, monthly trend, and subsidiary variance through the latest closed month.
- **Program-attributable ERP revenue:** The same analysis, but filtered through an approved item, segment, market, account, profit-center, or similar program mapping.

The business would probably want:

- Net Sales actual versus matching P&L Plan
- Plan attainment and variance amount
- YoY
- Monthly Actual versus Plan versus LY
- Subsidiary contribution and variance
- Optionally, a Gross Sales to deductions to Net Sales bridge
- Later, reconciliation between operational Sales and ERP financials

Technically, ERP should be a separate monthly fact table related through shared Date and Subsidiary dimensions. It must retain Version, scenario, currency, scale, close status, and available program-mapping fields.

The gating question is:

> Should ERP show total-subsidiary financial context, or only revenue attributable to the program?

My recommendation is total-subsidiary ERP Net Sales first, clearly labelled as financial context. Program ERP revenue should wait for an approved mapping.
